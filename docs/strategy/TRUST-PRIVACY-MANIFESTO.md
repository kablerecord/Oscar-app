## Why You Should Believe This

I don't expect you to trust words. Here's how I'm making trust verifiable:

### 1. Architectural Transparency

OSQR's privacy isn't a policy—it's how the system is built.

**The core principle:** *"OSQR stores everything. OSQR sees nothing."*

| What This Means | How It Works |
|-----------------|--------------|
| Your data is encrypted before it leaves your device | I hold ciphertext, not content |
| Embeddings generated locally | Your device creates the searchable index |
| User holds the keys | I cannot access your data even if I wanted to |
| Search happens locally | Queries never decrypted on my servers |

**The legal consequence:**
- Right to deletion? Delete your key—data becomes meaningless
- Subpoena? I can only hand over encrypted blobs
- Data breach? Encrypted data breach is a non-event
- "Sell my data"? I can't sell what I can't read

I'm not asking you to trust my intentions. I'm showing you a system where bad intentions wouldn't matter.

### 2. The Constitutional Framework

OSQR operates under a published constitution—rules the system follows that you can read and verify.

**What the constitution guarantees:**

- **User sovereignty**: Your data belongs to you. Period.
- **No surveillance capitalism**: OSQR will never sell data or attention
- **Transparency**: You can always see what OSQR knows about you
- **The Burn-It Button**: One click deletes everything. Actually deletes. Not "archived for 90 days."
- **No dark patterns**: OSQR will never manipulate you for engagement

These aren't policies. They're constraints built into the code.

### 3. Privacy Tiers You Control

| Tier | What It Means | Your Trade-off |
|------|---------------|----------------|
| **Local Only** | Data never leaves your device | No cross-device sync |
| **Encrypted Cloud** | Stored encrypted, you hold keys | OSQR can sync but can't read |
| **Platform Storage** | OSQR can index for better features | You trust the system |

You choose. Default is maximum privacy. You opt *into* sharing, not out of it.

### 4. Radical Receipts

I will publish regular transparency reports:
- Every data request received from governments or third parties
- What was shared (spoiler: nothing, because I can't read it)
- Any security incidents and exactly what was affected
- Revenue sources (proving no data monetization)

Boring consistency over time builds trust.

## What OSQR Will Never Do

These are hard commitments, not aspirational statements:

1. **Never sell your data** — The architecture makes this impossible, not just prohibited
2. **Never use your data to train models sold to others** — Your intelligence is yours
3. **Never show you ads** — Revenue comes from subscriptions and plugins, not attention harvesting
4. **Never lock you in** — Export everything, anytime, in usable formats
5. **Never manipulate for engagement** — OSQR's job is to improve your thinking, not maximize your screen time
6. **Never share with governments without legal compulsion** — And even then, I can only share encrypted data I can't read
7. **Never change these commitments** — The constitution is immutable. If I tried to change it, the system would be forked.

## Appendix: The Burn-It Button

One click. Everything gone.

Not "scheduled for deletion in 30 days."
Not "removed from active systems but retained in backups."
Not "anonymized and retained for analytics."

**Gone.**

The Burn-It Button:
1. Deletes your encryption keys from all devices
2. Instructs OSQR servers to purge all associated encrypted blobs
3. Removes all metadata
4. Confirms deletion with cryptographic proof

**Why this matters:**

You should be able to walk away. Completely. At any moment.

If you can't truly leave, you're not a user—you're a hostage.

OSQR will never hold your data hostage.
